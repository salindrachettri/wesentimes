A Rebel's Ink: Kabiranjan Saikia

Meet Assam's rebel poet and child prodigy, known for his revolutionary writings. Born in 1975 in Khutikata, Nagaon, Kabiranjan Saikia exhibited exceptional literary talent from a young age, penning his first notable work, "Aai Matri Assam," at just twelve. His writings passionately advocated for justice and the rights of the oppressed. Despite the prevailing climate of fear, Kabiranjan courageously criticized the United Liberation Front of Assam (ULFA) in an article for Ganatantra, showcasing his commitment to non-violence and his disapproval of bloodshed. Tragically, his life was cut short in 2000, but his legacy as a fearless poet and advocate for peace endures.

Kaito Sukhai: The World's Youngest General

Meet General Kaito Sukhai, born on May 22, 1933, in Ghukhuyi village, Nagaland. At just 22, he became the youngest general in the world, leading the Naga Safe Guards during the Naga independence struggle. Known for his strategic brilliance and fearless leadership, Kaito played a key role in pivotal battles against Indian forces. His legacy as a youth leader and revolutionary icon continues to inspire the Naga movement.
